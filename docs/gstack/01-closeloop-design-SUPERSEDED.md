## Problem Statement

Win the GStack/GBrain hackathon demo by shipping a sales/outreach + live-call copilot that does the whole GTM loop end-to-end:

1. **Find** the right people to talk to (HOG enrichment + deep-research).
2. **Reach out** with personalized messages grounded in the team's brain (GBrain memory + subagent drafting).
3. **During the call**, show a live copilot panel that knows: who the prospect is, what we've said in past calls, what worked, and what to say next (Groq Whisper streaming + GBrain hybrid search + ZeroEntropy rerank).
4. **Learn** from the call and propose the next move (GBrain `dream --phase synthesize` + auto-drafted follow-up).

The validation is operational, not theoretical: we book real demos with judges/partners during the hackathon, run one live, and the copilot is what makes us close. The demo on stage at hour 5:45 shows the funnel вҖ” outbound (30s), live call w/ copilot (90s), self-improving follow-up (30s), close + roadmap (30s).

## What Makes This Cool

- **The brain is the moat, not the UI.** GBrain already ships memory + synthesize + hybrid search + MCP + subagents. The live copilot is mostly a thin web client over MCP. Nobody else competing here can ship this leg because nobody else has GBrain's memory layer.
- **All three legs in one product.** Clay does outbound. Gong does conversation intelligence. Cresta does live whisper. Nobody does all three for a solo founder, grounded in their own knowledge base.
- **The "look what it learned" beat is real.** GBrain's existing synthesize phase + subagent runner generate the follow-up draft live on stage. Not theater.
- **Recursive: we use the product to demo the product.** Outbound to judges via our own outbound. Live call IS the demo. Follow-up email proposes the partnership.

## Constraints

- 5h 45m wall-clock from session start. Hard.
- Must use the GStack/GBrain hackathon stack: GBrain + HOG + ZeroEntropy + Groq + Modal (any subset that ships).
- Demo target: YC partners / hackathon judges. They're reachable via LinkedIn, hackathon Slack/Discord, and (sparingly) email/X DMs.
- Team is small. Two-person live-demo job (one on the call, one operating the copilot + slides).
- 3-minute stage demo. If a leg doesn't finish, it gets cut, not faked.

## Premises (agreed, revised by CEO review)

1. **The brain is the moat, AND the overlay is the product surface.** CEO review overrode the original "no Electron" premise. The product is "a brain that's invisibly there during your calls" вҖ” a browser tab can't be that. Electron with a transparent + always-on-top + click-through window that is EXCLUDED FROM SCREEN-CAPTURE is the actual product. The demo lands harder because the founder can turn toward the Zoom camera during the call and the prospect sees nothing on the screen-share вҖ” the brain stays private to the user. Build cost: ~2вҖ“3h. Paid for by dropping ZeroEntropy entirely and thinning the dashboard view (see Electron Scope below).
2. **Cold email in 5 hours is dead.** New-domain reply rates are ~0%. Real outbound for this hackathon is **LinkedIn DMs (primary)**, personal Gmail (for people whose email is public), and X DMs (long shot).
3. **Audio is the technical crux.** If audioвҶ’transcript doesn't work in the first hour, we cut the live-call leg and switch to typed-input demo mode. **Reviewer caught a critical error here in the first draft:** Groq's `/v1/audio/transcriptions` is **batch-chunked, NOT WebSocket streaming**. The realistic approach is **BlackHole** virtual audio device on Mac вҶ’ `MediaRecorder` (audio/webm) вҶ’ server-side ffmpeg transcode to WAV every 5s вҶ’ POST to Groq batch endpoint. Expect **2вҖ“4 second end-to-end latency**, not 300ms. If we need true sub-second streaming, swap Groq for **Deepgram realtime** or **AssemblyAI realtime** (true WebSocket). Decision threshold: if Groq's 2вҖ“4s chunked latency makes the on-stage suggestions feel laggy after rehearsal, swap to Deepgram in hour 4 вҖ” Deepgram realtime is a 30-line drop-in.
4. **GBrain's `dream --phase synthesize` IS the self-improving leg.** No new ML to build. Drop the live call's transcript into GBrain via `put_page`, run synthesize, the subagent drafts a follow-up referencing what was actually discussed.
5. **The 3-minute demo narrative drives the feature list, not vice versa.** Anything that doesn't show up in the narrative is cut.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Thin Triathlon (CHOSEN)
Effort: M / Risk: Med. All three legs live, mercilessly cut. Browser panel (not Electron). LinkedIn DMs (not cold email). Audio via BlackHole + Groq Whisper streaming with a typed-input fallback. Frontload risk in hour 0вҖ“1 with four parallel validations.

### Approach B: Pre-Recorded Bullet Train
Effort: S / Risk: Low. Only the live-call beat is live; outbound and self-improving legs are pre-recorded clean screen captures. Safer, less novel. **Reserved as the hour-2 fallback if Approach A's parallel validations fail.**

### Approach C: Maximalist (also-live cycle)
Effort: L / Risk: High. Adds a live `dream` cycle running on stage. Highest payoff for this specific hackathon (it IS the GBrain hackathon вҖ” running GBrain's cycle live is the most on-brand demo). Cut for 5h45m because it doubles integration time and adds a failure mode that's hard to recover from on stage.

### Approach D: Brain-first slow burn
Effort: M / Risk: Low product, High demo. 2 hours of pure brain seeding (every past call + pitch deck + soul/user docs), then 3 hours of UI + outbound. Cut because the booking leg slips to hour 5+ and we lose buffer for rehearsal.

## Recommended Approach: Thin Triathlon

**Hour 0 (NOW вҶ’ +60min) вҖ” Frontload risks IN PARALLEL.** If any validation fails, that leg is cut OR we switch to Approach B (pre-recorded) at the hour-1 checkpoint. Decision explicit, not silent.

- Build `bin/draft-outbound.ts`: input LinkedIn URL вҶ’ HOG enrichment вҶ’ GBrain subagent drafts personalized DM grounded in team's pitch + SOUL.md. Output: 3-line DM in a copy-paste format. Cost: 1 HOG call + 1 short Anthropic call per target.

  2. **Walk up to them at the venue** (highest yield, zero latency, build the DM draft in 30s and read it to them)

**Hour 2вҖ“4 вҖ” Live copilot panel.**
- Single-page web app, Bun-served from localhost.
  - **Profile pane:** HOG profile cached on first DM-draft; GBrain search for `people/<slug>` shows our notes.
  - **Transcript pane:** Groq Whisper streaming, ~300ms chunks. Diarize speaker turns. Highlight names + numbers + objection phrases via regex.
  - **Suggestion pane:** Every 5s, take last 30s of transcript вҶ’ GBrain hybrid `search` (filter by `wiki/calls/` + `originals/`) вҶ’ top-20 hits вҶ’ ZeroEntropy `zerank-2` rerank against the last assistant question or prospect statement вҶ’ Groq (`llama-3.3-70b-versatile` or whatever's fastest) generates a 1вҖ“2 sentence "say this" line citing the source page. Display all 3 with scores. Auto-refresh, no flicker.
- Keyboard `[1][2][3]` to pin a suggestion (logs to GBrain as a take on the prospect's page).
- `[T]` to toggle typed-input fallback mode if audio breaks.

**Hour 3:30вҖ“4:30 вҖ” LIVE DEMO CALL (pre-stage).**
- Run the actual booked 5-min call with a judge/partner. Two-person job: one talks, one operates the copilot.

- Immediately post-call, run:

Honest framing in the narration: "we ran this just before walking up; here's what it produced." Not theater вҖ” the work is real, the timing is rehearsed.

**Hour 4:30вҖ“5:15 вҖ” Build the on-stage replay path.**
- Wire the panel's "demo mode": replay the recorded call's audio file through the same transcript pipeline so the suggestions surface in front of judges with the EXACT timing they had during the real call.
- The follow-up email + extracted takes are pre-rendered; the panel reveals them with a 5s animation when the founder hits "End call."
- If the live booking at hour 3:30 FAILED (no judge available): use a recorded internal call with a teammate playing prospect. Disclose it briefly on stage ("we couldn't book a judge in time, here's an internal call from earlier"). Don't fake it.

**Hour 5:15вҖ“5:45 вҖ” Rehearsal + final buffer.**
- 3 dry-runs of the 3-minute narrative end-to-end. Time each one вҖ” if any run exceeds 3:30, cut a beat.
- Pre-record a 30s screen capture of EACH leg as a Plan B contingency.
- Final QA: are all secrets in `.env`? Is the brain seeded + embedded? Is the demo laptop battery charged? Is BlackHole still routing? Is the demo laptop on tethered hotspot (NOT hackathon WiFi)? Is the projector/HDMI tested?

### Electron Scope (added by CEO review)

**Two windows in one app.**

1. **Overlay window** (the in-call view):
   - `transparent: true`, `frame: false`, `alwaysOnTop: true`, `resizable: true`
   - Positioned right-edge of screen (default 480px wide), user-draggable
   - macOS-only-but-fine: `setContentProtection(true)` on the BrowserWindow вҖ” Electron's native call to NSWindow's `sharingType = .none`. Screen-share recordings and capture APIs see a blank space where the window is. ZOOM AND OBS RESPECT THIS.
   - Click-through mode toggled by `[T]` keyboard shortcut вҖ” when on, the overlay receives no mouse events, clicks pass through to the app behind. Off by default so the user can interact with suggestions.
   - Renders the 3-pane in-call panel: profile (left), transcript (center), top-3 suggestions (right).

2. **Dashboard window** (the out-of-call view):
   - Standard `BrowserWindow`, not transparent.
   - Opens on app launch by default, OR via tray icon, OR via keyboard shortcut from the overlay.
   - Shows: recent calls (last 10 transcripts with summarize-extracted takes), recent prospects (last 20 HOG-enriched cards from the brain), one "New outbound" button that opens the LinkedIn-URL-to-DM flow.
   - **Demo role:** the founder opens the dashboard at beat 1 of the demo ("here's where we live when we're not on a call"), then the overlay takes over for beats 3вҖ“4.

**Demo choreography for the screen-capture-exempt overlay:**

The HARD trick to land on stage: the audience sees the panel ON the founder's screen during the demo, BUT during the replayed call clip, the audience also needs to "see" the panel reacting. Two paths:

- **Path A:** Two displays вҖ” laptop screen (where the overlay is visible) mirrored to the projector via HDMI. The overlay shows up on both because both are the user's screens, not screen-share. Audience sees everything.
- **Path B:** During the replayed-call beat, the laptop has Zoom NOT actively screen-sharing вҖ” so the screen-capture exemption is irrelevant on the projector. The audience sees the overlay normally. The privacy-from-prospect angle is sold via NARRATION ("during the real call earlier, the prospect's screen-share of me showed nothing here вҖ” proven in the recorded video we cut into the demo"). Demonstrate the exemption with a 5-second cut from the recorded call where the founder's mouse hovers over Zoom's "share screen" button to show "even if I shared, this panel would be invisible to you." Then narrate that line as proof.

Path A is simpler and what we should plan for. Path B is the fallback if the projector path forces a single display.
